# Channel Playbook

## Landing Page Copy

Structure the page in this order:
1. Immediate problem recognition
2. Clear product promise
3. 2-4 specific examples of tasks or earning outcomes
4. Why join before launch
5. Signup CTA

Use this framework:
- Headline: concrete outcome
- Subheadline: who it is for and why it matters now
- Proof bullets: task examples, speed, flexibility, local relevance
- CTA: signup action
- Friction reducer: city priority, early access, co-creation, updates

## Blog Posts

Default blog structure:
1. Problem-first headline
2. Everyday scenario intro
3. Explain the hidden cost of the problem
4. Introduce Szybka Fucha as the practical answer
5. End with a signup CTA

Strong blog angles for clients:
- brak czasu na drobne naprawy
- zakupy i odbiory bez rozwalania planu dnia
- dlaczego małe zadania zabierają najwięcej energii
- jak odzyskać czas dzięki lokalnej pomocy

Strong blog angles for contractors:
- jak dorobić lokalnie bez wiązania się grafikiem
- elastyczne zlecenia dla studentów i osób po pracy
- jak budować reputację i wracać po kolejne zlecenia
- na co zwracają uwagę klienci, gdy wybierają pomocnika

Always place a CTA:
- near the top for high-intent readers
- at the end for readers who need more context

## Email

Recommended formats:
- Waitlist welcome
- Role-specific nurture
- City-launch update
- Market research follow-up
- Reactivation

Default email skeleton:
- Subject line
- Preview text
- Opening tied to one pain point
- 1 core promise
- 2-3 proof bullets
- Single CTA

## Ads And Social

Write in batches of variations rather than one polished line.

Vary by:
- audience: client vs contractor
- hook: time saved, convenience, money, flexibility, local access
- format: short, medium, testimonial-style, question-led

Useful short-form formulas:
- Problem -> solution -> CTA
- Desire -> friction removed -> CTA
- Question -> benefit -> CTA

## Conversion Heuristics

Prefer:
- one audience per asset
- one CTA per asset
- vivid task examples
- local specificity when available
- everyday verbs: zleć, znajdź, pomóż, zarabiaj, dołącz

Reduce:
- too many benefits in one sentence
- vague claims without examples
- overexplaining the app before naming the user problem

## Suggested Experiment Ideas

Good tests for this product:
- client-focused hero vs contractor-focused hero
- speed hook vs flexibility hook
- co-creation CTA vs early-access CTA
- city-priority CTA vs generic signup CTA
- problem-first headline vs benefit-first headline
